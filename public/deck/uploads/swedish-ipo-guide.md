# NDAP AB — Swedish IPO Marketplace Guide

> **Status:** Research Complete | **Last updated:** 2026-03-16

## 1. Executive Summary

Sweden offers three primary marketplaces for growth company IPOs: Nasdaq First North Growth Market, Spotlight Stock Market, and NGM Nordic SME. Multiple crypto/blockchain companies and over 100 crypto ETPs are already listed on Swedish exchanges, establishing clear precedent for NDAP.

**Recommendation: Dual-track approach.** Evaluate both Spotlight and First North in parallel during weeks 1-4. The final decision depends on: (1) CA feedback on case readiness, (2) whether a pre-IPO round is completed, and (3) investor appetite. Spotlight is the safer path for a pre-revenue venture case; First North is achievable if a pre-IPO round validates the case first.

### Decision Framework

```
No pre-IPO round → Spotlight (lower barrier, 3-6 MSEK cost)
                        → Build traction → Upgrade to First North

Pre-IPO round (15-30 MSEK) → First North directly (validated case, 6-10 MSEK cost)
```

**Critical insight:** Liquidity depends more on market maker + analyst coverage + cornerstone investors than on exchange choice. These must be secured regardless of which exchange is chosen.

### Key Differentiators
- **First North:** Most prestigious, largest investor base, highest shareholder threshold (300), requires ongoing CA
- **Spotlight:** Lowest shareholder barrier (100), Spotlight reviews directly, very active crypto ETP venue (100+ Valour ETPs)
- **NGM Nordic SME:** Revenue-sharing model (trading fees offset costs), mentor for 2 years only, smallest marketplace (~12 companies)

## 3. Recommendation: Spotlight Stock Market

### Why Spotlight for NDAP

**The core argument:** First North has become increasingly "bank-driven" — they prefer companies with stable revenues, multi-year track records, and established governance. NDAP is a pre-revenue venture case with a story-driven thesis. Spotlight is built for exactly this type of company.

| Factor | First North | Spotlight | NDAP Fit |
|--------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Company profile | Stable revenue, track record | Venture cases, frontier tech | **Spotlight** |
| Crypto/digital assets | Safello, Arcane, Hilbert | 100+ Valour crypto ETPs, BTCX | **Both** |
| Min. shareholders | 300 (EUR 500 each) | 100 (SEK 2,000 each) | **Spotlight** (easier) |
| IPO cost | 6-10 MSEK (realistic) | 3-6 MSEK | **Spotlight** |
| Process complexity | High, formal CA-driven | Simpler, Spotlight reviews directly | **Spotlight** |
| Investor culture | More conservative | More risk-tolerant, story-driven | **Spotlight** |
| Board requirements | Min. 3 | Min. 4, 2 independent | Comparable |

### Why Spotlight Specifically for NDAP
1. **Venture-friendly** — Spotlight is used to listing companies in blockchain, gaming, biotech, and frontier tech. NDAP's three-layer digital asset thesis fits this culture.
2. **Lower cost** — IPO costs of 3-6 MSEK vs 6-10 MSEK on First North. Critical when raising 75-150 MSEK.
3. **Lower shareholder threshold** — 100 shareholders vs 300. More achievable for a first listing.
4. **Simpler process** — Spotlight reviews applications directly (no external Certified Adviser required, though having one is recommended).
5. **Crypto ETP hub** — Spotlight hosts 100+ Valour crypto ETPs, making it the single largest venue for digital asset products. Swedish crypto investors already trade here.
6. **Story-driven companies** — Spotlight investors understand and accept pre-revenue companies with strong narratives. NDAP's three-layer thesis is exactly the type of clear structure that works here.

### The Upgrade Path
Many Swedish companies follow this trajectory:
1. **Start on Spotlight** — lower barrier, prove the business model
2. **Build traction** — revenue, governance, shareholder base
3. **Upgrade to First North** — when ready for institutional capital and higher valuation multiples

This is a well-established path in the Swedish market. Starting on Spotlight does not limit NDAP's long-term ambitions.

### When First North Might Be Better
- If NDAP secures strong institutional cornerstone investors who prefer First North
- If the Certified Adviser recommends First North based on valuation expectations
- If NDAP achieves revenue traction faster than expected before listing

### Why Not NGM
- Only ~12 listed companies — very limited visibility
- Limited investor awareness
- May signal lower quality

## 6. IPO Process — Step by Step

### First North Listing Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| 1. Decision & advisors | Month 1-2 | Board resolution, engage CA + legal + auditor. Convert to publikt AB. |
| 2. Due diligence & prep | Month 2-4 | Legal/financial DD, prospectus drafting, governance framework, Euroclear registration |
| 3. Application | Month 3-4 | Submit listing application to First North via CA |
| 4. FI prospectus review | Month 3-5 | Submit to Finansinspektionen (see timeline below) |
| 5. Marketing & roadshow | Month 4-5 | Investor roadshow, bookbuilding (2-3 weeks) |
| 6. Listing | Month 5-6 | FI approval, allocation, settlement, first trading day |

**Total: 4-6 months** (from decision to first trading day)

### Finansinspektionen Prospectus Review

Per EU Prospectus Regulation (2017/1129), Article 20:

| Stage | Duration | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| First review (new issuer) | 20 working days | FI reviews, issues comments |
| Company response | 2-4 weeks | Address comments, revise |
| Second review | 10 working days | FI reviews responses |
| Third review (if needed) | 10 working days | Rare |
| **Total typical** | **6-10 weeks** | 2-3 rounds |

- FI prospectus review fee: **65,000 SEK**
- EU Growth Prospectus available for SMEs (lighter disclosure)
- Prospectus validity: 12 months from approval

## 7. Publikt AB Requirements

| Feature | Privat AB | Publikt AB (publ.) |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| Min. share capital | 25,000 SEK | **500,000 SEK** |
| Min. board members | 1 + 1 deputy | **3 members** |
| VD (CEO) required | No | **Yes** |
| CEO as chairman | Permitted | **Not permitted** |
| Auditor required | Not always | **Always** |
| Can list shares | No | **Yes** |
| Can offer to public | No (max 200) | **Yes** |
| Transfer restrictions | Allowed | **Generally not allowed** |
| Name suffix | AB | **AB (publ.)** |

### Swedish Corporate Governance Code
Applies mandatorily to Nasdaq Stockholm Main Market and First North Premier. For standard First North, Spotlight, NGM: recommended ("comply or explain").

Key requirements:
- Majority of board independent of company and management
- At least 2 independent of major shareholders (>10%)
- Audit committee required (at least 1 member with financial expertise)
- Remuneration committee required
- Nomination committee required
- Annual corporate governance report
