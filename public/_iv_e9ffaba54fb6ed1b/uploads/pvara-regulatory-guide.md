# NDAP AB — PVARA Regulatory Guide

> **Status:** Research Complete | **Last updated:** 2026-03-16

## 1. Executive Summary

Pakistan has rapidly formalized its virtual assets regulatory landscape. The Pakistan Virtual Assets Regulatory Authority (PVARA) was established in July 2025 and permanently enshrined through the **Virtual Assets Act 2026** (passed March 2026). PVARA operates a three-phase licensing process, currently at the NOC stage. Binance and HTX were the first NOC recipients (December 2025).

**Key finding:** Pakistan ranks **3rd globally** in crypto adoption (Chainalysis 2025) with an estimated 30-40 million users and $300B+ in annual trading activity. No exchange currently holds a full operational license — creating a significant first-mover opportunity for the NBX-NDAP joint venture.

## 3. Three-Phase Licensing Process

### Phase 1: Preliminary NOC (No Objection Certificate)
- Disclose beneficial ownership and Ultimate Beneficial Owners
- Demonstrate AML/CFT policies aligned with FATF
- **Prove existing licensure in recognized major jurisdiction (US, EU, or Singapore)**
- Submit full business plan
- Upon NOC: can immediately begin AML-Registered Services (limited basis)

**Implication for NDAP:** NBX holds Norwegian VASP and e-money licenses under Finanstilsynet. Whether Norway/EEA qualifies under "EU" criterion must be confirmed with PVARA directly.

### Phase 2: SECP Registration & Local Presence
- Register with Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan
- Establish physical office in Pakistan
- Set up local subsidiary/corporate structure
- Register on anti-money-laundering system
- Enable tax compliance, on-site inspections, and enforcement jurisdiction

### Phase 3: Full Operational License
- Comprehensive cybersecurity protocol review
- Capital adequacy assessment
- Risk management systems evaluation
- Proof-of-reserves audits
- Segregated customer assets (mandatory)
- Insurance coverage for custody (may be required)
- Ongoing reporting obligations

### Operational Requirements
| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| AML/KYC | Full FATF compliance, suspicious activity reporting |
| Technology | Cybersecurity protocols subject to PVARA review |
| Proof-of-reserves | Audits required |
| Sharia compliance | **Non-negotiable** — Sharia Advisory Committee guidance |
| Customer asset segregation | Mandatory separation of client and operational funds |
| Fit and proper | Directors and key personnel must pass background checks |
| Company registration | Must be registered in Pakistan under Companies Act, 2017 |
| Local management | Local directors/management with relevant experience |

### Penalties for Non-Compliance
- Fines up to **PKR 50 million (~USD 179,000)**
- Imprisonment up to **5 years**
- Applies to unlicensed operations

## 5. Foreign Ownership Rules

### General Pakistan Foreign Investment Policy
| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Foreign ownership limit | **100% permitted** in most sectors |
| Virtual assets/crypto | **Not a restricted sector** |
| Legal framework | Foreign Private Investment (Promotion and Protection) Act |
| SECP registration | Required for foreign companies establishing operations |
| Companies Act, 2017 | Part XII governs foreign company compliances |

### Can a Swedish-Listed Entity Hold 50% of a Pakistani Exchange?
**Yes, in principle:**
- Pakistan permits 100% foreign ownership in most sectors
- 50/50 JV structure is well within permissible limits
- JV must be incorporated as a **Pakistani company under Companies Act, 2017**
- Foreign parent companies (NDAP AB, NBX) can hold equity in locally registered entity
- Must disclose all ownership details and Ultimate Beneficial Owners

### PVARA-Specific Foreign Requirements
- Must be **licensed in recognized major jurisdiction** (US, EU, or Singapore)
- Physical office in Pakistan mandatory (Phase 2)
- Full beneficial ownership disclosure
- **Action needed:** Confirm with PVARA that NBX's Norwegian/EEA license qualifies as "EU"

### SBP (State Bank of Pakistan) Stance
| Date | Position |
|------|----------|
| 2018 | Advised regulated entities to avoid dealing in virtual assets |
| May 2025 | Clarified virtual assets are "not illegal" but awaiting framework |
| Dec 2025 | SBP Governor met with PVARA chairman — active cooperation |
| Current | Virtual assets recognized as regulable asset class under PVARA. SBP collaborating on banking interface |

**Note:** SBP's 2018 circular has not been formally rescinded, though the Virtual Assets Act 2026 effectively supersedes it. Banking access for crypto exchanges remains a practical consideration.
